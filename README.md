# Cycle Adaptive Differential Oscillator (CADO)

The **Cycle Adaptive Differential Oscillator (CADO)** is an advanced, dynamically adjusting momentum oscillator written in Pine Script v6.

Traditional momentum oscillators, such as the MACD or standard Price Oscillators, rely on fixed lookback periods (e.g., 12 and 26 bars). These static inputs represent a rigid assumption about market behavior, often resulting in significant lag during trend transitions and whipsaws during consolidations. CADO eliminates this rigidity by continuously measuring the market's true **Dominant Cycle** and mathematically scaling its internal filters to stay perfectly in phase with current market rhythms.

By adapting its short and long smoothing periods to the live heartbeat of the market, CADO offers a highly responsive, lag-reduced oscillator that automatically tunes itself to changing volatility and timeframes.

---

## Methodology: Under the Hood

The indicator breaks down into four sequential mathematical components:

### 1. Dominant Cycle Estimation

The indicator continuously evaluates price action using a Hilbert Transform to estimate the market's current dominant cycle. The measured cycle is constrained between user-defined minimum and maximum limits to maintain structural stability and filter out erratic data spikes.

### 2. Adaptive Harmonic Lengths

Once the Dominant Cycle is identified, CADO does not use it directly as a single moving average length. Instead, it creates two distinct periods using a user-defined harmonic spacing ratio (`Cycle Range`):

* **Short Period:** Derived by dividing the dominant cycle by the cycle range.
* **Long Period:** Derived by multiplying the dominant cycle by the cycle range.

### 3. SuperSmoother Filtering

To eliminate the lag associated with standard Simple or Exponential Moving Averages, the price data is passed through a two-pole **SuperSmoother Filter (SSF)** using both the calculated Short and Long periods. This results in two incredibly smooth, low-lag synthetic price curves.

### 4. Normalized Differential Oscillator

Finally, the oscillator is generated by calculating the normalized difference between the Short and Long SuperSmoother filters:
`(Fast - Slow) / (Fast + Slow) * 100`

This normalizes the momentum output into a percentage-based format, keeping the oscillator scaled and readable regardless of the asset's underlying price.

## For Traders: How to Read & Use

CADO plots as an area-style oscillator below your main price chart, oscillating around a central Zero Line. Because its lengths are dynamic, it tends to be much more decisive than standard oscillators.

**Trend Regimes (Zero-Line Crossovers)**

* **Bullish Regime:** When CADO crosses above the Zero Line (value > 0), short-term momentum has overtaken the macro structural trend. The indicator area will shift to the designated **Up** color, signaling a high-probability bullish environment.
* **Bearish Regime:** When CADO crosses below the Zero Line (value < 0), the market is displaying structural weakness. The indicator area will shift to the designated **Down** color, signaling bearish momentum.

**Momentum Expansions & Contractions**

* The amplitude (height or depth) of the oscillator represents the velocity of the trend. A rising, steep CADO indicates accelerating momentum, while a flattening or reverting CADO suggests the current cyclical push is exhausting.

**Divergences**

* Because CADO is fundamentally rooted in cycle analysis, standard oscillator divergences (e.g., price making a higher high while CADO makes a lower high) are particularly potent, often signaling an impending cyclical reversal.

## ⚙️ Settings Guide

| Setting | Description | Recommendation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Source** | The price data used for the underlying calculations. | **hl2** (Median Price) is the default and highly recommended for cycle analysis, as it captures the true midpoint of every bar's action. |
| **Min Dominant Period** | The absolute lowest cycle length (in bars) the estimator will look for. | **8** is the default. Lowering this makes the algorithm hyper-reactive to noise. |
| **Max Dominant Period** | The absolute highest cycle length (in bars) the estimator will look for. | **48** is the default, capturing most standard cyclical movements. |
| **Cycle Range** | The harmonic multiplier/divisor used to separate the fast and slow filter lengths. | **2.0 (Full Octave)** is the default for clear structural separation. Try **1.618 (Golden Ratio)** for a more organic, slightly tighter response, or **1.414 (Half-Octave)** for aggressive scalping. |
| **Colors (Up/Down)** | Controls the visual rendering of the oscillator and its accompanying area plot. | Adjust to match your chart's aesthetic. The default uses subtle gray/brown tones for a clean look. |
